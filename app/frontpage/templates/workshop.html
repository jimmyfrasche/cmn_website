Neuroimaging on the browser: Embracing web technology for more open and collaborative science

Anisha Keshavan, Monday, August 14th. Location TBD.

Dr. Keshavan is an enthusiastic member of the brain hack community, contributing to three out of 5 of the featured projects from this year's OHBM hackathon. She received her PhD in Bioengineering from University of California - San Francisco and is joining the University of Washington as a Post-Doc. Her open source projects include:

  • MindControl, an app for quality conrol of neuroimaging pipeline outputs (Demo)
  • Medulina, an app for crowdsourcing manual MRI tracing tasks.


How not to fool yourself with p-values and other statistics

Regina Nuzzo, Date and Location TBD.

Dr. Nuzzo's writing has appeared in (links follow) the New York Times, Los Angeles Times, ESPN the Magazine, Reader's Digest, Scientific American, New Scientist, Science News, and Nature, among others. She was a guest on NBC's Today show (video) in 2008 to discuss her popular LA Times article on evolutionary psychology and dating. Her feature article on p-values earned her the American Statistical Association's 2014 Excellence in Statistical Reporting Award, and she facilitated the working group that produced the precedent-setting 2016 ASA P-Values Statement.
